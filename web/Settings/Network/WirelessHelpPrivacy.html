Two ways that a wireless network can protect your data are Wireless Equivalent Privacy (WEP) and Wireless Protected Access (WPA). You choose which one of these to use on your wireless network by configuring your wireless access point or router. Older devices only support WEP which is not as secure as WPA. If a device supports WPA and WEP, it is best to choose to use WPA unless you need to maintain compatibility with WEP for other devices which connect to the network.
